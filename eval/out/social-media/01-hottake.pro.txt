Ok here's my actual take on mechanical keyboards. They're worth it, but not really for the reasons people always push on you.

Everyone goes on and on about build quality and durability and how the whole thing is some kind of investment. And sure, that's all fine and it's probably true. But that's honestly not the reason I'd tell you to go get one. The real reason is just the typing itself. A regular membrane board feels like you're pressing your fingers into wet mush, and the weird thing is you don't even notice how much you hate it until you finally switch to something better. Then going back to the old one feels kind of gross.

And then yeah, there's the whole customizing rabbit hole. You can swap out the keycaps and the switches basically forever if you want to. That's either the best part of the hobby or it's a total money pit, and which one it ends up being really depends on how much self control you happen to have. Mine, apparently, is completely gone. I've got three of these boards sitting on my desk now and I really only need the one.

So would I tell you to get one? If you type all day for work, then yeah, no question about it. If you're more of a casual user sitting on the fence, you'll honestly be fine either way. But you'll probably end up wanting one anyway.
