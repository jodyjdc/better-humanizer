The following is my considered assessment of mechanical keyboards as a category. They represent a worthwhile upgrade for nearly any computing setup. There are several distinct reasons that support this particular conclusion.

Build quality is the first important consideration for any prospective buyer. Mechanical switches consistently deliver a tactile typing experience that membrane boards cannot adequately replicate. The available customization is the second consideration worth noting here. Users are able to replace the keycaps and the switches to suit their individual preferences. The overall durability is the third consideration that buyers should evaluate carefully. A well constructed board can reasonably be expected to last for many years of use.

These combined factors make mechanical keyboards a sensible choice for most users. They improve general comfort during extended typing sessions at a desk. They also tend to improve measured productivity for many different working professionals. A mechanical keyboard is therefore a reasonable investment for the average person. It will likely improve the way that you approach your daily work.
