SAN FRANCISCO (Reuters) -- Norvell Systems reported $3.1 billion in quarterly revenue on Wednesday, beating analyst expectations, as growth in its cloud-computing division lifted profit 28 percent from a year earlier.

The technology company said net income rose to $640 million for the quarter, up from $500 million in the same period last year. Revenue from the cloud division grew 41 percent and accounted for nearly half of total sales, according to the company's statement.

The results beat most forecasts. "The cloud business is now the main driver of the company's growth," said Priya Anand, an analyst at Brightline Capital who follows the firm. She told Reuters the pace of that growth would be difficult to sustain at the same rate, and that investors should expect it to slow.

The company flagged weaker demand in some overseas markets and said currency movements had reduced international revenue. It did not give a specific figure.

Norvell shares rose about 6 percent in after-hours trading following the report. The gains pared earlier in the session. The company is scheduled to hold a call with investors on Thursday and will issue full-year guidance then, it said.
