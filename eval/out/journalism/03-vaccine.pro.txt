BOSTON (AP) -- Researchers at Hartwell University said Monday that an experimental vaccine sharply reduced infections from a common respiratory virus in an early-stage clinical trial, though they cautioned that larger studies are still needed.

In the trial, the vaccine lowered infection rates by about 70 percent among participants who received it, compared with those given a placebo, according to the study. The trial enrolled roughly 400 adults and ran over a single respiratory season.

"These are early results, and we're not ready to call it a success yet," said Dr. Helen Ruiz, who led the research team. "We need to confirm this in a much larger group." She told the Associated Press that a larger trial was planned for next year.

The virus is a frequent cause of seasonal respiratory illness, particularly in young children and older adults. There is currently no widely used vaccine for it.

The researchers said the vaccine appeared safe in the trial, with most side effects limited to soreness at the injection site. "It's an encouraging first step," Ruiz said. The findings have not yet been published in a peer-reviewed journal, the university said.
