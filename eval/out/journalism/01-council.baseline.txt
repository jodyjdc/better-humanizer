So here's the big news. The Riverside City Council just voted on a huge waterfront plan. It passed 5-2 on Tuesday. The price tag? A cool $42 million.

There's a lot packed in here. A new riverside park. Affordable housing. A public market too. It's a pretty big swing for a downtown that's been quiet for a while.

The plan could bring in jobs. Lots of them, supposedly. Some folks pushed back on the cost. That's fair. But plenty of people seem excited about it.

Crews should break ground next spring. City leaders sound upbeat about where things are headed. And really, who can blame them?

It's a big moment for the city. Downtown's been waiting a long time for something like this. Now it's finally happening.
