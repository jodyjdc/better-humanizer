RIVERSIDE (Staff) -- The Riverside City Council voted 5-2 on Tuesday to approve a $42 million plan to redevelop the city's downtown waterfront, clearing the way for construction to begin next spring.

The plan calls for a riverside park, a block of affordable housing and a public market on a stretch of downtown that has sat largely vacant for years, according to the proposal approved by the council.

Supporters said the project could create several hundred construction and permanent jobs. "This is an investment in a part of the city that has been waiting a long time," Mayor Carla Mendez said after the vote.

The two dissenting members questioned the cost. Councilmember Dale Hooper said the city had not fully accounted for long-term maintenance of the new park and market, and he pressed for a detailed operating budget before any contracts are signed. He voted no.

City officials said construction is expected to start next spring and take about three years to complete. The council is scheduled to review the project's financing plan at its meeting next month, the city said.
