"package does not exist" means Maven compiled against a dependency it can't find on the classpath — the jar never made it into your local repository, or the coordinates in pom.xml are wrong.

Check pom.xml first. Confirm the dependency block is there, and that the groupId and artifactId exactly match the ones on Maven Central; a single typo in the artifactId is the usual cause, and Maven won't warn you, it just resolves nothing.

Then force a refresh:

    mvn clean install -U

The -U flag tells Maven to update snapshots and re-check remote repositories instead of trusting the stale local cache.

If that still fails, the cached entry is probably corrupt. Delete it and let Maven re-download:

    rm -rf ~/.m2/repository/com/example/yourlib

Don't nuke the whole ~/.m2/repository unless you have to — re-downloading every dependency for every project is slow. Re-run the build after deleting just the one folder.
